The two arguments mentioned disagree on their opinion of the most efficient voting system. While the first text thinks, that traditional voting leads to inaccurate counting of votes and far too much errors caused by marking the wrong name, the second doubts that a computerized system would perfectly replace the current one. 

Consulting the first text's arguments computerized systems could remove the possibility of human errors and releave the people who have to count every single vote. On the other hand the contrary text reveals the aspect of discourage of voting, if the people aren't used to or don't have the possibility to use a computer in their daily life. 

An important point mentioned by both sides is, that either the traditional and the computerized system are programmed and developed by human beings. It's natural that there are errors being made, the concluding question is, which one are more serious?
