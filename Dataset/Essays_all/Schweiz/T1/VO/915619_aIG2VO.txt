Voting

Currently in the United States, people vote for a candidate on a sheet of paper, which contains many names. This type of voting system is inefficient and often lead to inaccurate counting of votes. Should it be replaced with a more efficient and trustworthy computerized voting system? There a few points, which are for and against this replacement.

Firstly, the computerized voting machines are easy to use and it is less inevitable that the voters will make mistakes, by marking the wrong candidate.

Secondly, they would not have to count every single ballot, because all the vote counting will be done quickly and automatically by computers.
