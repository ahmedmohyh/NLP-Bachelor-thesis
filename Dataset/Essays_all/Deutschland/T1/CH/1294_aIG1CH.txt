The Chevalier de Seingalt (1725-1798) wrote a memoir of his own life and experiences. The memoir is a good source of the european society in the 18th century. The memoir also has critics. For example the Chevalier was a wealthy man but he sometimes borrowed money from a Swiss merchant. Critics are asking themselves why he borrowed money although he was rich? The accuracy of the memoir is critized by the critics because he wrote about conversations between him and a famous writer named Voltaire after they already happened a lot of years earlier. The critics doubt that the Chevalier could remember his conversations so well. In his memoir it says that he escaped from the prison in Venice but many critics are sure that he had politically well-connected friends who were able to help him out of that situation.
