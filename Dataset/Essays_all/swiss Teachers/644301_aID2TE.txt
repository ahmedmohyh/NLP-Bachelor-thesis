There is no doubt that both, a teacher with empathy as well as a teacher with extensive knowledge is needed.
But the opinions differ when it comes to the question of which component is more important to be a successful teacher.
Arguments that speak for empathy are: There is no point in trying to teach anything if it won't reach the other side  due to the lacking connection between the teacher and the kids. School is also a place to grow as a human being and therefore, the teacher should be able to offer more than superior knowledge, because Wikipedia is capable of that job as well.
On the other hand, school is primarily a place to learn and if school doesn't provide teacher with striking knowledge, it might restrict the kids in their learning process. Especially smart children will soon notice, when their supervisor isn't able to answer questions that lay beyond the current curriculum.
